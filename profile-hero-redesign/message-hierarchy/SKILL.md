# Skill: Message Hierarchy

## Purpose

Rewrite and organize the hero message so visitors understand the value proposition quickly and feel guided toward the next action.

This skill turns vague or generic copy into a sharper, more memorable hero structure.

## Primary Job

Create a clear content hierarchy:

1. Role or identity label
2. Strong headline
3. Supporting subheadline
4. Short value explanation
5. CTA
6. Proof or trust statement

## When To Use

Use this skill after the hero diagnosis is complete, especially when:

- the headline feels broad or generic
- the value proposition is not obvious
- the CTA copy is weak
- the role labels are hidden or unclear
- the page does not answer “why should I care?”
- the page needs stronger conversion copy without becoming hypey

## Required Inputs

- Hero diagnosis from Skill 01
- Target audience
- Main outcome the person helps people achieve
- Current headline and supporting copy
- Desired CTA
- Tone of voice
- Available proof or credibility
- Any words/phrases the brand wants to keep

## Copy Principles

Use these rules:

- Make the promise specific.
- Keep the headline easy to scan.
- Use one visually emphasized word or phrase.
- Put the outcome before the process.
- Make the CTA action-oriented.
- Keep proof near the CTA.
- Avoid empty phrases like “unlock your potential” unless made specific.

## Quality Checklist

Before completing this skill, confirm:

- [ ] The headline can be understood in 3 seconds.
- [ ] The target audience can tell the page is for them.
- [ ] The promise is more specific than the original.
- [ ] The CTA feels obvious and relevant.
- [ ] The proof supports the desired action.
- [ ] Only one phrase is recommended for visual emphasis.

## Red Flags

Avoid:

- Generic motivational language.
- Too many headline ideas without a clear recommendation.
- Long paragraphs in the hero.
- CTA text that is vague, such as “Learn More,” unless intentionally appropriate.
- Emphasizing multiple words and weakening the focal point.
