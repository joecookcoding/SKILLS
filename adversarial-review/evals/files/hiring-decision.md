# Decision: Hire a Dedicated DevRel Engineer for Our Open-Source SDK?

## Context

- We make an open-source TypeScript SDK that wraps a payments API. ~1.2K GitHub stars, ~180 weekly npm downloads.
- 8-person engineering team. No marketing or DevRel hires today; the founder writes occasional blog posts.
- Burn: ~$140K/month. Runway: 11 months at current MRR ($62K).
- Last fundraise: 18 months ago. We are between rounds.

## Proposal

Hire one senior DevRel engineer at $180K base + 0.4% equity to:

- Triage GitHub issues (avg time to first response is currently 6 days).
- Write tutorials, docs, and example apps.
- Run a Discord community.
- Speak at 2-3 conferences per year.

## Stated Motivation

- The founder is the only person writing content and is burned out.
- Two prospective enterprise customers cited "lack of docs" as a blocker.
- A competitor recently hired a well-known DevRel and saw a measurable bump in adoption.

## Alternatives Briefly Considered

- Contract a part-time tech writer for ~$3K/mo.
- Have each engineer rotate weekly to triage issues and write one tutorial.
- Do nothing and rely on community contributions.

## Out of Scope

- Hiring outside the US.
- Hiring multiple people.
- Pausing other open roles (we already have a senior backend role open).
